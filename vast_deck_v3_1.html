16 / 25Project 1 — Lessons
What I learned. What I’d do differently.
Lessons learned
  • Multi-agent coordination is harder than single-agent augmentation. The General’s task assignment logic needed three full rewrites before producing consistent fleet behavior.
  • SPARTA v3.2 coverage is uneven. OT and CCSDS-specific paths required me to extend the taxonomy with my own mappings.
  • The crew safety flag emerging unprompted raised a harder question. How do you formally specify safety boundaries for an autonomous agent in a life-critical environment?
What I’d do differently
  • Define formal safety constraints before expanding the attack fleet. Emergent safety reasoning is promising but not sufficient at scale.
  • Build the Intel fleet earlier. The General’s reasoning quality is capped by the richness of Scout’s intelligence.
  • Invest in the Judge agent from day one. Compliance documentation as an automatic byproduct is what makes this viable in a CMMC-regulated environment.

The problem no one has solved yet.
Why existing approaches fail for a crewed space station
  • Commercial space stations sit at the convergence of OT (life-safety), IT (corporate/cloud), and AI/ML (autonomy, telemetry) — no existing red team playbook covers all three simultaneously.
  • Signal latency between Haven Station and ground ranges from 250ms to 8 minutes — a nation-state adversary at machine speed doesn’t wait for ground response.
  • A crew of 4–6 cannot maintain continuous security monitoring alongside mission operations. The station needs autonomous detection and response.
  • Standard EDRs reboot to patch. Haven-1 cannot reboot on demand — 90 seconds without attitude control means no power, no comms, no life support monitoring.
  • Nation-state adversaries are already watching. Haven-1 launching makes Vast a named target, not a theoretical one.

22 / 25Brilliant Idea — AIRAGKey slide
AIRAG
Autonomous Incident Response & AI Guardian — the 5th crew member. Its only job is Haven-1’s Digital Immune System.
Vertical integration argument

Because Vast builds its own hardware and software, AIRAG can be baked into the VCAP avionics kernel — not monitoring the network, but monitoring the voltage of the logic. If a command to the CMGs doesn’t match the physics model of the station, AIRAG kills it in the buffer before hardware moves.

Physics as root of trust

Standard security looks for bad code. AIRAG looks for bad physics. By integrating flight dynamics models into the AI, a “software patch” that would cause an unauthorized change in station attitude is recognized as a cyber-kinetic attack. Orbital mechanics is the final firewall.

The 5th crew member

Haven-1 has 4 crew. While they sleep, AIRAG runs continuous SPARTA-mapped simulations using the AEGIS framework to find and patch logic flaws. When the crew wakes up, the attack surface is smaller than when they went to bed.

AIRAG is the evolution of AEGIS. It takes the SPARTA reasoning from my laptop and moves it into the station’s RTOS. It doesn’t just find the gap — it autonomously deploys the OPA gate to close it. In orbit. Without a reboot. Without calling home.
